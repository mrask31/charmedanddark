# Editorial Layer Specification

**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: Locked  
**Last Updated**: February 5, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

The editorial layer exists to provide context, history, and cultural reference for objects, practices, and concepts within Charmed & Dark. It adds gravity without persuasion.

Editorial must never:
- Promote products or drive purchases
- Direct users toward Sanctuary or commerce
- Persuade, instruct, or guide behavior
- Use marketing or SEO language
- Simulate AI voice or personalization
- Ask questions of the reader

Editorial may inform or contextualize, but must never persuade, instruct, or direct action.

---

## 2. Editorial Role Definition

### What Editorial Is

Editorial content provides:
- Historical context for objects or practices
- Cultural or symbolic background
- Material or craft information
- Origin or provenance details
- Observational commentary on aesthetics or use

### What Editorial Is Not

Editorial is not:
- Product marketing
- Lifestyle guidance
- Spiritual instruction
- Purchasing advice
- Sanctuary promotion
- SEO content
- Conversion optimization

### Relationship to Other Realms

- **Commerce (Threshold)**: Editorial may reference objects but must not promote them
- **Sanctuary**: Editorial may reference practices but must not direct users there
- **Brand**: Editorial is separate from brand voice and marketing copy

---

## 3. Placement & Access

### Where Editorial May Appear

Editorial content may exist in:
- Dedicated editorial pages (e.g., `/editorial/[slug]`)
- Optional expandable sections on product pages (collapsed by default)
- Standalone articles or essays (linked from navigation)

### Where Editorial Must Not Appear

Editorial must not appear:
- In product descriptions (commerce copy)
- In checkout flow
- In post-purchase touchpoints
- In Sanctuary pages (Mirror, Grimoire)
- In navigation CTAs
- In hero sections or above-the-fold content

### Access Patterns

- Editorial is optional and must be clearly marked as such
- Editorial must not block or interrupt commerce or Sanctuary flows
- Editorial must not be required reading to complete any action
- Editorial must be accessible via direct link or explicit navigation choice

---

## 4. Allowed Formats

### Text

- Essays (500-2000 words)
- Short contextual notes (100-300 words)
- Historical references
- Material descriptions
- Cultural observations

### Visual

- Archival images (historical reference)
- Material close-ups (texture, craft detail)
- Diagrams (construction, origin)

### Prohibited Formats

- Video (too engaging, too directive)
- Audio (too immersive, too personal)
- Interactive elements (quizzes, polls, calculators)
- Animated content
- Embedded social media
- User-generated content

---

## 5. Tone & Language Constraints

### Required Tone

- Neutral
- Observational
- Factual
- Restrained
- Non-directive

### Prohibited Tone

- Persuasive
- Instructional
- Aspirational
- Emotional
- Marketing-driven
- SEO-optimized

### Language Rules

Editorial must:
- Use third-person or passive voice
- State facts without interpretation
- Provide context without judgment
- Reference sources when applicable
- Avoid superlatives and absolutes

Editorial must not:
- Use second-person ("you")
- Ask questions of the reader
- Use imperative voice ("consider", "imagine", "try")
- Use emotional language ("beautiful", "powerful", "transformative")
- Use marketing language ("perfect for", "ideal", "must-have")
- Use urgency language ("now", "today", "limited")

### Example: Acceptable

"The use of black candles in European folk practice dates to the 16th century. Black wax was produced by adding lampblack to tallow or beeswax. The color held no inherent symbolic meaning in most traditions."

### Example: Not Acceptable

"Imagine lighting a black candle in your space. You'll feel the weight of centuries of tradition. This powerful ritual object is perfect for those seeking to connect with ancient practices."

---

## 6. Interaction Rules

### No Calls to Action

Editorial must not include:
- "Shop now" or "Buy" links
- "Learn more" buttons that lead to commerce
- "Join the Sanctuary" prompts
- "Sign up" or "Subscribe" forms
- Social sharing buttons
- Comment sections

### Allowed Links

Editorial may include:
- Links to other editorial content
- Links to external historical or cultural sources
- Links to product pages (neutral, non-promotional)
- Links to home page or navigation

### Link Constraints

Links to products must:
- Be plain text (no buttons or CTAs)
- Use neutral language (product name only)
- Not imply recommendation or endorsement
- Not use urgency or scarcity language

Example: Acceptable
"The Gilded North Star Trio is an example of cast iron starburst design."

Example: Not Acceptable
"Shop the Gilded North Star Trio to bring this tradition into your home."

---

## 7. Relationship to Products & Sanctuary

### Products

Editorial may reference products to provide context, but must not:
- Recommend or endorse products
- Suggest products solve problems or fulfill needs
- Compare products to alternatives
- Highlight product benefits or features
- Use product names as marketing copy

### Sanctuary

Editorial may reference Sanctuary practices (Mirror, Grimoire) to provide context, but must not:
- Direct users to enter the Sanctuary
- Suggest Sanctuary membership is beneficial
- Describe Sanctuary as transformative or necessary
- Use Sanctuary as a conversion goal

### Commerce

Editorial must remain separate from commerce:
- Editorial pages must not include shopping carts or checkout
- Editorial must not be used to drive product discovery
- Editorial must not be optimized for conversion metrics

---

## 8. Never-Change Rules

The following rules must never be violated by future contributors:

1. **No Persuasion**
   - Editorial must never attempt to persuade, convince, or influence behavior
   - No calls to action, no directives, no instructions

2. **No Marketing Voice**
   - Editorial is not marketing copy
   - No promotional language, no SEO optimization, no conversion goals

3. **No AI or Personalization**
   - Editorial must not simulate conversation or intelligence
   - No personalization based on user data or behavior
   - No dynamic content based on user context

4. **No Questions to Reader**
   - Editorial must not ask questions of the reader
   - No rhetorical questions, no prompts for reflection

5. **Neutral Tone Only**
   - Observational, factual, restrained
   - No emotional language, no aspirational framing

6. **Optional Access**
   - Editorial must never be required reading
   - Editorial must not block or interrupt flows
   - Editorial must be clearly marked as optional

7. **No Interaction**
   - No comments, no sharing, no engagement metrics
   - No interactive elements or user input

8. **Separate from Commerce**
   - Editorial must not drive purchases
   - Editorial must not optimize for conversion
   - Editorial must not be used as a funnel

---

## Enforcement

This specification is locked. Any proposed change that violates these rules must be rejected.

If editorial content is unclear, default to omission.

If a piece of content requires persuasion, instruction, or direction, it is not editorial.

Editorial may inform or contextualize, but must never persuade, instruct, or direct action.
